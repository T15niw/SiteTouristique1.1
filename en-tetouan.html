A glipmse into Tetouan's history

Tetouan, Nestled in the Rif Mountains; often referred to as the "White Dove", has a rich history that reflects a blend of cultures. Founded in the late 15th century by Muslim refugees fleeing the Reconquista in Spain, it quickly became a cultural and trade center in northern Morocco. The city flourished under the Marinid dynasty, which enhanced its architectural heritage.
Tetouan also played a key role in the Spanish Protectorate era in the early 20th century, which has left a unique European influence on its urban landscape culture and architecture.

The city’s medina, a UNESCO World Heritage site, showcases a blend of Andalusian and Moroccan architectural styles, characterized by its narrow streets, vibrant markets, and stunning mosaics.
Today, Tetouan is known for its historic charm, bustling markets,strong traditions in craftsmanship and artistic heritage, making it a fascinating destination for travelers seeking a deeper connection to Morocco's diverse heritage and cultural tapestry.
